System Modules

CO-CREATING THE ENVIRONMENT THAT ENABLES FINANCIAL LIFE

The Advanced Algos System is being co-created by a group of development teams distributed around the world. It is a collaborative effort by which teams come together and agree on the basic organizational matters that enable the collective to tackle the development and maintenance of a large and complex system.

The system is subdivided in over 40 modules, thus, only a few teams contributing early on will be able to participate in this role. In order to achieve maximum resilience, teams develop a few modules each. We are an open project and you are welcome to join in and contribute the development of one module.

The project allocates 15% of the total supply of ALGO tokens to System Development. Each module is assigned a fraction of that percentage as a bounty. The precise allocation depends on the module's category, which is a function of the level of criticality, urgency and difficulty. There are five categories, each with a bounty, as shown below.

Each of the modules are estimated to require roughly 1 to 2 months of development for one senior developer working on a full time basis or a similar time for a team of two developers working part time. Should your team count with more resources, it shouldn't be a problem to deliver the module much faster.

Teams are autonomous and may freely decide how to organize their work internally. However, due to the fact that development starts from loose definitions and because teams are interdependent stakeholders within the ecosystem, we do require teams to work under an agile development approach in which batches of functionality are delivered in 2-week-long sprints.

Under such framework, teams interact with the project's core team and with other teams handling related modules to create definitions at the start of each sprint, and to evaluate results, assist integrations and coordinate testing at the end of each sprint.
