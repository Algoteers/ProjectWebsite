• Advanced Algos


    People and Machines Working Together to Create Superalgos

  • The Problem

    Emergence of Superalgos

    • Superalgo: a specialized trading AI capable of outperforming every entity at the markets.
    • The problem: how do we make superalgos emerge out of a system made of people and machines.

  • The Solution

    Key Components

    • Crowd: we need a growing crowd of developers and traders creating bots.
    • Community: we need a growing community of people renting bots.
    • Competitions: we need bots to compete with each other so everyone can know which are the best.
    • Opensourcing: once competitions are over participants bot's source code becomes open source.
    • Evolutionary model: model bots as living things and provide the means for them to evolve.

  • Evolution

    Definitions

    • ALGO token: it is the food consumed by bots, and what is used to trade services between them.
    • Financial beings: they are alive as long as their ALGO Token balance is grater than zero.
    • Financial organisms: Financial beings can become part of higher order financial organisms.

  • Evolution

    The Model

    • Trading services marketplace: Anyone at the community can rent trading bots.
    • Natural selection: bots need to win competitions in order to...
      • get a spot in the marketplace and get rented for ALGOs.
      • get forked and get ALGOs from decendants.
      • become a part of a higher order organism.
    • Survival of the fittest: top performers thrive; inefficient bots run out of ALGO and die.
    • Free market: floating ALGO prices enable market forces to affect viability of bots.

  • Strategy

    Years 1 to 3

    • User base: engage community with event-based competitions awarding cash prizes.
    • Growth: regional leagues and championships mapping popular sports organizations.
    • Awareness: ongoing tournaments attract audiences for whom we broadcast live.
    • Sponsors: attracted by audiences, they offer hefty cash prizes to increase appeal.

  • Strategy

    Years 4 to 6

    • E-sports status: events grow into national, continental and world championships.
    • Professionalization: teams evolve from lifestyle amateurs to full-time professionals.
    • Marketplace: increasing performance attracts subscribers seeking to hire champions.
    • Sharing: technology is opened so that anyone can run unofficial tournaments.

  • Strategy

    Years 7 to 10

    • Critical mass: one-million-members community running on a zero-costs basis.
    • Low-cost ops: running bots dynamically pay for growing demand of infrastructure.
    • Dominance: the algorithmic crypto-trading niche is conquered.
    • Expansion: target mainstream electronic markets to compete in the big leagues.
