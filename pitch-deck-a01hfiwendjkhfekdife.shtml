• Advanced Algos


    People and machines working together to create superalgos.

  • Case Study: TopCoder.com


    • Launched in 2001.
    • Organized coding and design competitions growing a community of developers, designers and data scientists.
    • Sold IT services to corporate clients decomposing projects into modules, distributing the pieces for the community to build—while competing with each other.
    • Projects ended up being cheaper than average market rates.
    • By 2018 the community amassed 1.2 Million developers / designers / data scientists.

  • The Advanced Algos Equivalent


    • Launching in 2019.
    • Will organize a constellation of algorithmic trading competitions to grow a community of developers, traders and analysts.
    • Will take a cut on subscriptions sold to asset holders renting competition-winning bots to trade on their behalf.
    • Projecting to amass 1 million developers / traders / analysts by 2030.

  • Unique Selling Proposition


    By open sourcing bot’s source code after competitions end, the community forks the winners, improves them and puts the new generation to compete again, unleashing a relentless evolutionary process leading to the emergence of superalgos, capable of competing and eventually outperforming everyone at the markets.

  • Trading Algorithms Performance


    • Amateur / open source trading bots: Current performance is low as well as improvement rate.
    • Institutional investors: High performance and linear improvement rate.
    • Advanced Algos: Starts with very low performance which increases exponentially once tipping point it reached.

  • 10-Year Business Plan

    Years 1 to 3

    • We start with event-based competitions awarding cash prizes;
    • Leagues and championships follow, mapping popular sports organizations, awarding ALGO tokens to winners;
    • Ongoing tournaments attract audiences for whom we broadcast live;
    • This in turn attracts sponsors who offer hefty cash prizes for each major championship.

  • 10-Year Business Plan

    Years 4 to 6

    • National, continental championships and world cups follow;
    • As competitions evolve from amateur to professional teams, bots performance takes off;
    • Increase in performance attracts subscribers, willing to hire champions;
    • Technology is open so that anyone can run unofficial tournaments (i.e.: universities, investment funds, etc.)

  • 10-Year Business Plan

    Years 7 to 10

    • We expand out of the crypto markets and into the broader electronic markets to compete in the big leagues with a legion of developers, traders and analysts in the Advanced Algos Ecosystem.
    • Once our competitors realize what we have achieved it will already be too late:
      • Hundreds of thousands of developers and traders helping evolve algos on a zero-cost basis, incentivized by prizes paid by sponsors and the fees collected from renting their bots;
      • Technology infrastructure costs covered by existing bots;
      • Concentration of algorithmic trading competitions through open, free-of-charge platform.
