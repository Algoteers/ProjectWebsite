  • Advanced Algos

    Our Case

    • Launching in 2019.
    • Organizes a constellation of algorithmic trading competitions to grow a community of developers, traders and analysts.
    • The community trains bots and puts them to compete for hefty prizes.
    • Projecting to reach 1 million developers, traders and analysts by 2030.

  • Evolution

    Value Proposition

    By open sourcing bot’s code after competitions end, the community forks the winners, improves them and brings the new generation back to compete again, unleashing a relentless evolutionary process leading to the emergence of superalgos capable of outperforming everyone at the markets.

  • Superalgos

    • Amateur / Open Source: Current intelligence is low as well as improvement rate.
    • Institutional Investors: High intelligence and linear improvement rate.
    • Advanced Algos: Starts with very low intelligence which increases exponentially.

  • Business Model

    Platform as a Service (PaaS)

    • Advanced Algos runs an economic ecosystem in which different stakeholders collaborate to generate value.
    • The ecosystem’s output is a collection of ever-evolving trading organisms whose collective intelligence grows exponentially.
    • The platform serves an open Automated Trading Services Marketplace through which investors can hire World Champion bots to trade on their behalf.
    • Trading happens entirely at third-party exchanges, the funds never leaving subscribers’ own accounts.

  • ALGO Token

    Utility

    • Payments: ALGO is the medium of payment within the platform by which bots autonomously pay microfees for the services they consume and earn microfees for the services they offer.
    • Energy: The ecosystem connects ALGO tokens to algorithms as a form of energy essential to their life sustainability, turning bots into beings with a financial life of their own.
    • Technology: An ERC-20 Token on Ethereum.

  • Financial Beings

    Living Autonomous Agents

    • Algorithms within the platform are autonomous agents, which –after being released in the open—become living stakeholders in the ecosystem.
    • Financial life makes them behave in ways conducive to protecting their own interests: they strive to produce value in order to get ALGO payments for their services.
    • Bots need ALGO to stay alive. Good performers thrive through the income they generate, while inefficient bots run out of ALGO and die.

  • Strategy

    Years 1 to 3

    • We start with event-based competitions awarding cash prizes;
    • Leagues and championships follow, mapping popular sports organizations, awarding ALGO tokens to winners;
    • Ongoing tournaments attract audiences for whom we broadcast live;
    • This in turn attracts sponsors who offer hefty cash prizes for each major championship.

  • Strategy

    Years 4 to 6

    • National, continental championships and world cups follow;
    • As competitions evolve from amateur to professional teams, bots' performance takes off;
    • Increasing performance attracts subscribers, seeking to hire champions;
    • Technology is opened so that anyone can run unofficial tournaments (i.e.: schools, universities, investment funds, etc.)

  • Strategy

    Years 7 to 10

    • By the time we reach one million developers, traders and analysts, we expand out of the crypto markets and into the broader electronic markets to compete in the big leagues.
    • Once big-league players notice our presence it will be too late, because:
      • Our one-million-members community runs on a zero-cost basis, incentivized by prizes paid by sponsors and the fees collected from renting their bots;
      • Our technology infrastructure costs are covered by running bots;
      • We dominate the algorithmic trading competitions space.
